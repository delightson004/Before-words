Before Words

Some stories don't begin with a conversation.

Some begin before you understand anything at all.

Some begin before you're even born.

There were two families.

Bound not by accident —

but by something older than memory.

A friendship that survived distance.

A connection that quietly stitched itself

through years, through prayers,

through dreams that were told out loud

and then gently handed to God.

Even before I arrived in this world,

something was already being written.

I just didn't know yet

that I was one of the characters.

I saw her sometimes.

The way you notice a star —

not because you went looking,

but because it was simply there.

Always there.

We never really spoke.

We didn't need to, back then.

She was just someone who existed

at the edges of my life —

like a song playing in another room

that you never fully hear,

but somehow never forget.

Then one day, I found something.

A photograph.

Old. Small. Almost forgotten

between the pages of a life

that had kept moving without explaining itself.

When I asked about it —

the answer arrived quietly.

No drama. No thunder.

Just a truth

that rearranged something inside me

so gently

I didn't even notice

until it was already done.

And then there was a day —

just an ordinary afternoon —

when someone said something casual.

Unaware of its own weight.

Unaware of what it would become.

A sentence.

Not a love letter.

Not a confession.

Just words —

that landed somewhere in me

like a seed lands in soil.

Silent.

Still.

But already becoming something.

From that day,

I started seeing differently.

Moments passed between us like seasons — Each one small, each one unspoken, Remembered only by me, perhaps. Left unsaid, the way real feelings often are. In every room we ever shared, Nearest to each other, yet furthest apart. Silence was the only language between us — Held carefully, like something fragile. And still I kept returning to the edges of it, Returning to moments no one else even noticed. It was never a choice, really. Nothing about this ever was. All I know is — I couldn't look away. Distance came, as it always does. And life moved forward without asking permission. Rooms emptied. Seasons changed. Time kept going. And yet — something in me stayed still. That something had no name. Had no explanation. It had only ever had a face.

She went away to build her.

I stayed and tried to build mine.

We existed in the same world

but rarely in the same room.

And when we were —

I spoke to everyone around her.

She spoke to everyone around me.

And somehow, in all that noise,

in all that closeness —

we remained the only two people

who never really spoke to each other.

Maybe we didn't need words yet.

Maybe the story wasn't ready for that chapter.

Then came an evening.

At the turn of a year.

She said two words

without looking at me.

And then —

just before leaving —

she looked.

Just once.

I don't know if she knows

what that one look did

to someone who had been

quietly collecting

every small moment

she never noticed she was giving.

I haven't said anything.

Not directly.

Not yet.

But somewhere in a photograph

turned to art,

two people rendered in colour and silence —

somewhere in these words,

in these lines —

I have been saying it.

The only way I knew how.

Quietly.

Patiently.

The way you wait for someone

when you believe

the story was written

long before either of you

arrived to read it.

Maybe she never finds this.

Maybe she does.

Maybe she reads this and feels nothing.

And maybe that's okay too —

because some things deserve to be said

even when you don't know

if anyone is listening.

But maybe —

Maybe she reads this

and something stirs.

Something quiet.

Something she can't quite name.

Maybe she reads this and remembers

that some stories

don't start with a conversation.

They start before that.

Long before that.

And maybe —

just maybe —

they're still being written.
